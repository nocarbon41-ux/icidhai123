## Conference Overview

### About ICIDHAI 2026

The **International Conference on Innovations in Digital Humanities through AI (ICIDHAI-2026)** aims to critically examine the transformative impact of artificial intelligence on language, literature, and cultural studies. It provides a global platform for scholars, researchers, and practitioners to engage in interdisciplinary dialogue bridging humanities and computational sciences.

The programme aims to explore how artificial intelligence in literary and linguistic studies while fostering interdisciplinary collaboration between scholars in the humanities and computational fields. It seeks to explore emerging computational research methodologies and critically address issues of authorship, originality, and ethics in technology-assisted scholarship.

The programme also promotes integrated pedagogy and contributes to curriculum innovation and policy discourse in higher education. As an outcome, the conference will provide a platform for interdisciplinary scholarly dialogue, foster research collaborations and academic networking, and facilitate the publication of selected papers in the Rupkatha Journal on interdisciplinary studies in Humanities.

Additionally, all accepted papers will be published in conference proceedings with an ISBN, contributing to the broader discourse on integrating artificial intelligence into higher education systems. With AI transforming how knowledge is produced and interpreted, this conference positions humanities scholars at the forefront of technological discourse. It fosters critical engagement with digital culture, ethics, and evolving academic practices.

### Conference Objectives

- Promote theoretically grounded research on AI and Digital Humanities
- Encourage critical engagement with emerging technologies in literary and linguistic studies
- Examine questions of authorship, originality, and academic integrity in technology-assisted research
- Address ethical concerns surrounding algorithmic mediation, surveillance, and digital governance
- Strengthen collaboration between scholars, practitioners, and policy thinkers working at the intersection of AI and the humanities
- Contribute to curriculum innovation and policy discourse in higher education institutions adapting to AI-driven transformations
